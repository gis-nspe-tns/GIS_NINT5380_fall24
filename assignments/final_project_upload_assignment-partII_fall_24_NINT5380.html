Final Project Upload Instructions - Part II (Peer Review)

During the first week of December (Monday December 2nd - Sunday December 8th), student final projects are prepared in draft format for the first submission by Sunday December 8th at 11:59pm.

The directions for this draft submission are located in the submission assignment titled Final Project Deliverable

In addition to this primary deliverable, you will also submit a slightly different version of your draft submission to the assignment titled Final Project Deliverable - Part II (Peer Review).

This ‘Part II’ requires that you export and save locally first your methods and then your presentation as separate, unique PDF files. These two (2) files are then uploaded to the Canvas assignment Final Project Deliverable - Part II (Peer Review). When you complete your submission, you will be prompted to peer review three (3) other student submissions - both their methods overview paper and presentations in their draft forms prior to finalization Sunday December 15th, 11:59pm.

Peer Review Guidelines

For your three (3) peer reviews of fellow student projects, your identity will be anonymous and you will be assigned the three (3) projects on a random basis. The goal of the peer review is to provide helpful, concise feedback that can be used by the student whose work you are reviewing for constructive revision or additions prior to project finalization on the 15th of December.

While you are free to form your review as you wish, the following guideline prompts can be used to target your review:

  1. Is the presentation inviting and compelling? Do you as the viewer have an quick understanding of the project location, project goals and the GIS analysis that has been accomplished in the project?

  2. Is the presentation and methods complete and coherent? Is it clear from the methods overview and presentation that full consideration has been given to the critical aspects of the project - data gathering, data organization, GIS analysis and a results summarization?

  3. Are there particular sections or components of the project that seem unclear or unfocused? Are there analysis steps that seem unrelated to the larger goal(s) of the project?

  4. Are there presentation elements that could be improved in design and/or cartographic approach?

In the end, you want to provide ‘actionable’ feedback that will allow your fellow students to quickly isolate components of their draft materials that may need revisions or additions. Try to avoid providing only generalized comments - either positive or negative - that are subjective or untargeted to particular issues in the methods overview and presentation. Certainly you can provide generalized summarization of your likes/dislikes, but make sure you have particular points that again are ‘actionable’ for your fellow student to revise or add before their final submission, December 15th.
